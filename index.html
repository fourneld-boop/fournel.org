Future Scenarios & Long-Range Worlds

This page is the hub for speculative futures on fournel.org. It links detailed crisis modelling in World War III, a long-range Bahai-inspired future, and supporting timelines in history, technology, and religion. None of this is prediction – it’s a lab for testing how different assumptions about politics, tech, and faith might play out.

Meta-Timeline: How the Futures Branch

This isn’t another detailed timeline – it’s a simple “spine” that shows where the WW3 and Bahai futures diverge, and how tech.htm, history.htm, and religion.htm connect to both.

2025–2040 · Shared Pre-Crisis World

Common starting conditions for all scenarios

  • Climate stress, technological disruption, and geopolitical rivalry dominate. Both ww3.htm and bahai.htm assume a turbulent, multi-polar world.
  • tech.htm provides the capability floor: AI, automation, bioscience, space infrastructure, and cyber tools are maturing rapidly.
  • religion.htm and history.htm provide the long memory: past empires, reform movements, and religious transformations shape how societies interpret the crisis.

2040–2070 · Divergence: WW3 vs. Managed Crisis

Two main branches

  • WW3 branch: In ww3.htm, deterrence fails, conflict escalates into a global war, and the international system is forcibly rebuilt after catastrophic loss.
  • Bahai branch: In bahai.htm, crises stop short of all-out war. Painful lessons, near misses, and partial breakdowns drive states toward a pragmatic Lesser Peace.
  • Both branches draw on the same tech envelope (tech.htm) but use it differently: in one case as a weapon set, in the other as a tool for coordination and monitoring.

2070–2150 · Post-Conflict Orders

Reconstruction, reform, and global architecture

  • After WW3 (ww3.htm): Post-war architecture is heavy on security guarantees, demilitarisation, and enforced constraints. A new world order emerges out of trauma.
  • Bahai pathway (bahai.htm): The world commonwealth evolves more incrementally, formalising a World Super-State with layered authority and stronger emphasis on consultation and justice.
  • history.htm is relevant here: both orders echo past attempts at empires, federations, and leagues – but with higher stakes.

2150–3000 · Long-Range World Order

Where bahai.htm becomes the main spine

  • Beyond roughly the mid-2100s, bahai.htm carries the primary long-run storyline: Great Peace, deeper spiritual civilisation, and early attributes of the Most Great Peace.
  • tech.htm bounds what is physically and technically plausible in those centuries – including potential off-world infrastructure.
  • religion.htm suggests how religious traditions, including the Bahai Faith, might inform ethics, law, and culture in such a world, even as new movements appear.

Beyond 3000 · Open Scenario Space

Not covered here (yet)
