*Caption2: Nap.sr. came West in 1908, to check things out first for 4 months.

He stayed at Simone’s farm near VilleNeuve, Alta. He then brought

the family out West. They stayed in St. Albert before going to Colinton, Alta.,

homestead, in 1912.While in St.Albert, Nap. sr. fixed shoe’s for the GreyNuns. Henri took over the homestead at Colinton. Marie Jeanne tells a story, about how her sister Julliette saved her from drowning. Marie Jeanne had borrowed her brother

Charles’s small axe to cut a hole in the ice covering Pine Creek. The axe fell thru the hole. When she tried to retrieve the axe, she fell in, her sister Julliette pulled her out.

Another story Marie Jeanne relates to Julliette suffering from Croup, and having

problems breathing. Their father gave her a spoonfull of Coal Oil and Sugar. This made her cough up some mucous. Coal Oil today would be poisonous.

In 1917 they moved to Winterburn, Alta. The Larue’s were operating a Creamery.

Nap. sr. bought a farm here from Mrs. Tough at Winterburn.
