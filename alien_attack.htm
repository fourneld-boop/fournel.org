Hostile First Contact · 2026

“Your World Is Now Ours”

In this science-fiction branch, a signal from a star 130 light-years away reaches Earth in 2026. It is broadcast into our networks in dozens of languages, simultaneously: “YOUR WORLD IS NOW OURS.” Within a year, a vast armada that has clearly been in the outer Solar System for decades steps out of hiding and begins a technologically realistic takeover: orbital bombardment, drone occupation, hackable infrastructure – no magic, no hand-waving faster-than-light travel, just the consequences of being late to notice that someone else has been here first.

Core Assumptions

  • The radio message left their system ~1896; they have known about Earth for over a century.
  • A slow-generation fleet arrived in the outer system long before 2026, hiding among comets and dark objects, using low-signature propulsion and cold, stealthy hulls.
  • The 2026 declaration is psychological – not the start of their journey, but the moment they reveal a conquest that was already underway.
  • The aliens are technologically ahead, but not godlike: they use physical weapons, network attacks, advanced robotics, and logistics we can at least comprehend.

This branch focuses on a practical takeover from 2000–2100, and can be read alongside tech.htm for capability envelopes and ww3.htm for human-only great-power stress tests.

Timeline: 2000–2100 (Alien Attack Branch)

Early years quietly build the space and network infrastructure the invaders will later hijack. The break comes in 2026–2027, when the declaration arrives and the hidden armada steps into the open.

2000–2015 · Setting the Stage (Without Knowing It)

  • 2000–2007: GPS, satellites, and global fibre networks knit the planet into a single tightly coupled system. What’s good for commerce will later be good for occupation.
  • 2008–2012: Drones, precision weapons, and network-centric warfare concepts mature in human militaries. Our doctrines assume we control the orbital high ground.
  • 2013–2015: CubeSats and commercial launches proliferate. Space becomes crowded with small, cheap, poorly defended hardware whose telemetry and protocols are visible to any quiet observer in-system.

2016–2025 · The Unseen Fleet Waits

  • 2016–2019: Deep-space surveys pick up anomalies in the outer Solar System – odd thermal signatures in the Oort Cloud and scattered Kuiper Belt objects. They are written off as calibration errors or unexplained astrophysical curiosities.
  • 2020–2022: AI-assisted sky surveys quietly flag a small set of objects with suspiciously stable relative positions and unusual reflectivity. Papers are drafted, peer-reviewed, then buried in noise; no one has a budget to follow up properly.
  • 2023–2025: Deep-space probes are proposed but delayed. Geopolitics, pandemics, and climate concerns keep attention firmly on Earth. At the edge of the system, an alien armada slowly adjusts its orbits, matching inclinations and phasing itself for inward transfer burns timed for the late 2020s.

2026 · The Message

  • January 2026: Radio telescopes detect a narrowband signal from a known star ~130 light-years away. Before the usual verification protocols can complete, the same signal appears embedded in terrestrial infrastructure – hijacking satellite downlinks, digital billboards, streaming services, and emergency broadcast systems.
  • The message is simple, repeated in dozens of human languages and symbolic logics: “YOUR WORLD IS NOW OURS.”
  • Analysis shows the embedded terrestrial copies are being injected by unknown transmitters in high orbits, piggybacking on our own constellations. Someone is already here.
  • Governments attempt to take networks offline; the message keeps reappearing for several hours, then abruptly stops. The original deep-space signal continues as a faint, steady beacon – a time-stamp from 1896 saying: “we knew you were here, and we’re coming.”

2027 · The Armada Reveals Itself

  • Early 2027: Infrared and radar sensors, now on high alert, finally pick up large objects decelerating from the outer system on high-energy transfer orbits – thousands of kilometres of metal and composite structures riding on fusion or beamed-power drives, shedding waste heat through huge, faint radiators.
  • They were never 130 light-years away. The radio message was a psychological opening salvo and a receipt: the fleet has been here for years, possibly decades, coasting in the dark.
  • Mid 2027: The armada brakes into the inner system, using Jupiter and other planets for gravity assists. A wave of smaller craft detaches and accelerates toward cislunar space, matching orbits with GPS, communications, and surveillance satellites.
  • Late 2027: Without firing a shot, the aliens begin physically capturing and modifying human satellites, attaching parasite modules that can spoof signals, shut down coverage, or feed false data. Ground controllers see glitches, resets, and telemetry that “doesn’t make sense.”

2028 · Orbital Strangulation

  • January–March 2028: Major spacefaring nations attempt to launch defensive interceptors and anti-satellite weapons. Many launch vehicles fail mysteriously at critical points in flight – guidance corrupted, telemetry jammed, or upper stages detonated by precise kinetic hits from tiny, previously unseen alien drones.
  • April 2028: The first overt attack: several massive rods of dense metal – essentially alien “Rods from God” – are released from high orbits, de-orbiting onto uninhabited test ranges and deserts. The message is clear: they can hit anywhere, with nuclear-like energy, from orbit.
  • May–June 2028: Global position systems and large portions of communications constellations are seized or spoofed. Ships drift off course, aircraft scramble to revert to inertial navigation, and financial networks suffer cascading outages.
  • July–December 2028: A ring of alien platforms takes up station at Earth–Moon Lagrange points and high, near-polar orbits, establishing layered coverage of the entire planet. From there, they can observe, jam, and strike with minimal delay.

2029–2032 · Planetfall and Local Control

  • 2029: The first atmospheric entries: hypersonic autonomous craft that skip along the upper atmosphere, mapping radar coverage, power grids, data centres, and military bases in exquisite detail.
  • 2030: Coordinated kinetic strikes disable key airbases, naval ports, ICBM silos, and hardened command facilities. Instead of glassing cities, the aliens decapitate military capacity, leaving civilian populations largely intact – valuable labour and biological resources.
  • 2031: Low-orbit carrier platforms begin dropping swarms of ground drones: quadrupeds, tracked vehicles, and small hovering units. They establish perimeters around critical infrastructure – power plants, ports, industrial hubs – enforcing no-go zones with non-lethal but overwhelming force (directed-energy dazzlers, area-denial microwaves, high-velocity projectiles).
  • 2032: Cities are not occupied street-by-street by alien troops; instead, machine cordons and airborne patrols segment regions, allowing human authorities to manage day-to-day order under alien surveillance. Compliance is rewarded with restored power and limited network access; resistance is punished with precision strikes on specific blocks, not wholesale destruction.

2033–2040 · Resistance, Adaptation, and Exploitation

  • 2033–2035: Human militaries and resistance networks adapt:
    • reverting to fibre and line-of-sight comms where possible,
    • using analogue and air-gapped systems for planning,
    • developing EMP devices and cyber exploits targeting captured satellites and ground drones.
  • The aliens respond by tightening control over orbital assets and deploying more robust, hardened drones that can operate with minimal external comms, relying on local sensing and pre-loaded mission trees.
  • 2036–2038: Resource extraction ramps up: the armada establishes permanent facilities on the Moon and in near-Earth space, refining regolith and captured debris into construction material and fuel. Earth’s industrial base is coerced into supporting this build-out.
  • 2039–2040: Environmental manipulation begins: targeted aerosol injections, orbital shades, and marine machinery are used to nudge climate and ocean chemistry in directions that appear marginal for humans but ideal for alien biochemistry and long-term infrastructure stability.

2041–2060 · The Occupation Matures

  • 2041–2045: New political entities emerge:
    • “Client regimes” that explicitly cooperate with alien directives in exchange for relative autonomy and tech crumbs, and
    • underground city-states and rural networks that survive by staying below orbital detection thresholds.
  • 2046–2050: Hybrid human–alien infrastructure appears:
    • energy grids patched with alien storage and transmission tech,
    • transport routes optimised for alien logistics, not human convenience,
    • medical and life-support systems derived from alien biotech, but distributed only to compliant populations.
  • 2051–2060: Human science advances by reverse-engineering captured or “lost” alien hardware, particularly in materials and control systems. A quiet arms race develops between alien security AIs and human hackers, fought in the dark corners of compromised networks and repurposed machine vision systems.

2061–2080 · Cracks in the Armada

  • 2061–2065: Long-range astronomy finally resolves part of the picture: we detect faint, relativistic exhaust plumes from other alien fleets elsewhere in the galaxy. It’s clear we’re not the only system being pacified.
  • 2066–2070: Maintenance gaps appear. Some orbital platforms show signs of deferred repair; others manoeuvre into “graveyard” orbits and go dark. Either the home civilisation is stretched thin, or this is what their normal occupation decay curve looks like.
  • 2071–2075: Coordinated human resistance efforts time attacks for windows when local orbital coverage is thinnest. Several ground installations are recaptured, yielding intact alien reactors, drives, and fabrication systems.
  • 2076–2080: Evidence emerges – via intercepted control traffic and degraded alien transmissions – that the armada is a second-tier colonial asset, not the apex of their capabilities. The homeworld may be dealing with other crises, or simply considers Earth “secured enough” to divert attention elsewhere.

2081–2100 · Toward a Post-Conquest Earth

  • 2081–2088: The alien presence shifts from active conquest to semi-automated management. Many orbital nodes are now run by long-lived machine systems following high-level policies with minimal real-time oversight from the origin civilisation.
  • Humans exploit this automation: inserting noise into sensor networks, subtly altering local policy parameters, and gradually carving out zones of de facto autonomy that look compliant from orbit.
  • 2089–2095: A new strategic choice confronts humanity:
    • attempt a risky, large-scale revolt that might trigger devastating orbital strikes, or
    • continue the slow erosion of alien control, aiming to inherit much of their infrastructure when the armada eventually departs or collapses.
  • 2096–2100: The scenario ends in a tense equilibrium:
    • Earth is formally “theirs,” criss-crossed by alien-built infrastructure and guarded by semi-autonomous orbital weaponry.
    • But pockets of human society have grown adept at living in the blind spots, co-opting technology, and planning for the day when the lights in the alien command cores finally dim.
    Whether that day comes through patient decay, external war in the alien’s own region of space, or a decisive human uprising is left for the next scenario file.
