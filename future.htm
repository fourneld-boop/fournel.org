Timelines · Scenarios · Branching Futures

Future timelines branching out from the present.

This page collects the explicitly future-focused timelines on fournel.org: branching stories about AI defence networks, alien contact and invasion, world-order evolution inspired by the Bahai Faith, and futures that grow out of alternative trajectories for the Second World War. None of these are predictions – they’re ways of stress-testing our assumptions about what comes next.

How to read these timelines

Each page is a separate “branch” of the future:

  • skynet.htm – AI defence network and escalation risk.
  • alien.htm – cautious first contact in 2026.
  • alien_attack.htm – hostile armada and planetary takeover.
  • bahai.htm – staged world order evolution to 3000 AD.
  • ww2.htm – futures that emerge from an altered World War II path.

For context, history.htm and tech.htm provide the deep-time background for both politics and technology.

Scenario Index

Where these branches overlap

Even wildly different futures share some structural beats. This quick meta-timeline shows how the scenario pages relate to one another and where they diverge.

Shared backdrop · 20th century foundations

All futures, including those in ww2.htm, rest on long historical currents captured in history.htm and technological arcs from tech.htm. In some branches, World War II plays out as in our timeline; in others, its outcome and institutions shift, changing the base conditions for AI, space, and global governance.

2000–2035 · The convergence zone

All five future timelines pass through an early-21st-century world of climate stress, digital networks, and unevenly distributed AI. Around the mid-2020s: Skynet begins as a defence modernisation project (skynet.htm); alien signals appear in either cooperative or hostile form (alien.htm, alien_attack.htm); and the first building blocks of a Bahai-style Lesser Peace begin to show up in bahai.htm.

2035–2100 · Divergence and crisis

In some branches, automated defence and misaligned incentives drive escalation and near-disaster (Skynet). In others, the main disruption is external – a watching civilisation or an occupying armada. The Bahai timeline assumes severe tests but avoids permanent collapse, using shocks to move slowly toward more integrated world governance. WW2-based futures can amplify or dampen these shocks depending on how mid-century institutions evolved.

2100–3000 · Long-term trajectories

Beyond roughly 2100, bahai.htm carries the clearest forward story, but Skynet, alien contact, alien attack, and WW2-derived futures all feed into potential long-run outcomes: different balances between central authority and local autonomy, different uses of high technology, and different levels of contact with any non-human intelligences that share the universe with us.
